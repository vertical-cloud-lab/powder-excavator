## Notes on peer-reviewed usage relevant to battery/ceramics/additive-manufacturing R&D
### Ball milling / powder mixing (MSK-SFM-3; MSK-SFM-3-F)
The MSK-SFM-3 is explicitly reported as a “high-speed vibrating milling machine” used for dry ball-milling CoFe2O4 and BaTiO3 powders for magnetoelectric composite processing (a powder-mixing workflow common in ceramics and functional oxide R&D). (samghabadi2021scalablecostefficientsynthesis pages 3-5)

The MSK-SFM-3 is also used for mechanochemical synthesis workflows in lithium niobate powders, including use of nylon vials and YSZ balls and staged milling to avoid overheating—details that matter for cohesive powders and heat-sensitive chemistries. (sanchezdena2019determinationofthe pages 3-5)

A related variant, MSK-SFM-3-F, is reported as a “high-energy 3D ball mill” operated at 500 rpm for 30 min using a 500 mL stainless-steel jar and specified milling media/ratios; the paper includes a photograph of the mill (Figure 1b). (ramirezarellanes2025pozzolanicassessmentof pages 2-4, ramirezarellanes2025pozzolanicassessmentof media deda1eb5)

### Tape casting
Ceramic tape casting for multilayer ceramic capacitor (MLCC) prototyping is explicitly reported using a “Compact Tape caster, MTI,” supporting MTI’s relevance in ceramic green-tape processing workflows (which depend on slurry mixing and powder dispersion upstream). (wang2019cold‐sinteredc0gmultilayer pages 1-5)

### High-throughput powder dispensing / weighing and integrated processing lines (battery R&D)
An MTI-authored review of high-throughput LIB materials synthesis describes an automated solid-powder dispensing concept: “four or more powder dispensing heads and balances” integrated with a carousel-type sample changer, preparing up to 32 samples with ≤10 g per sample and 0.01 g resolution. The same source describes linked high-throughput milling/mixing, pressing, and coating modules intended to move beyond thin-film combinatorics into gram-scale powder and slurry workflows closer to coin-cell fabrication. (liu2017highthroughputmaterials pages 7-11, liu2017highthroughputmaterials pages 1-7)

## Gaps relative to the requested MTI product catalog
The retrieved literature does **not** provide (and thus this answer cannot evidence-back) the following for most MTI powder-handling products: (i) specific dispensing mechanisms (auger vs vibratory vs screw feeder vs loss-in-weight), (ii) powder cohesiveness/particle-size operating envelopes, (iii) control/automation interface standards (RS-232/USB/Modbus/Ethernet/API), (iv) glovebox-specific configurations, and (v) MTI-listed price tiers. These are typically only in MTI datasheets/quotations rather than peer-reviewed methods sections. (liu2017highthroughputmaterials pages 7-11, liu2017highthroughputmaterials pages 1-7)
