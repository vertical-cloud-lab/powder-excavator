# Auger slices (v3 — two-part design)

The auger is now **two printable parts** (per PR review): a FIXED inner
shaft carrying the helical fin, and a ROTATING outer tube driven by the
spindle. Each part is sliced independently for the project's two FDM
rigs from PR #7 §6 (Original Prusa MK3S+ + Creality Ender-3) on 1.75 mm
PLA at 0.4 mm nozzle / 0.2 mm layers / 3 perimeters / 40 % gyroid infill
/ 4 mm brim / supports at 50°.

## Files

|---|---|---|---|---|
| FIXED shaft | MK3S+    | [`auger-shaft.MK3S.gcode`](auger-shaft.MK3S.gcode)       | ≈ 1 h 42 m | 11.12 cm³ |
| FIXED shaft | Ender-3  | [`auger-shaft.Ender3.gcode`](auger-shaft.Ender3.gcode)   | ≈ 1 h 35 m | 11.12 cm³ |

| ROTATING tube | Ender-3 | [`auger-housing.Ender3.gcode`](auger-housing.Ender3.gcode) | ≈ 2 h 17 m | 25.25 cm³ |
| FIXED shaft (USB short-name) | Ender-3 | [`SHAFT.gcode`](SHAFT.gcode) | same as Ender-3 above | 11.12 cm³ |

Print the two parts separately (back-to-back is fine — same filament,
same bed/nozzle temps).

### CuraEngine (Ultimaker Cura's open-source slice engine)

Generated by `slice_cura.sh` when `cura-slicer` is available on the
runner. Same naming pattern with `.cura.gcode` suffix:

```
auger-shaft.{MK3S,Ender3}.cura.gcode
```

(See PR-comment table for Cura-specific time / material — Cura runs
~10 % faster / leaner than PrusaSlicer for the same nominal settings,
identical to the v2 monolithic results documented in earlier comments.)

## Print orientation & supports

- **Shaft** (`auger-shaft.*.gcode`) — print **M3 stud DOWN on the bed**
  (Z+ = shaft tip). The flat top of the stud is the only good
  first-layer face; the helix overhangs at ~18° (printable on FDM
  without supports), and the bottom-end taper of the shaft is at 45°
  (also support-free). 4 mm brim around the stud's small bed contact.
- **Housing** (`auger-housing.*.gcode`) — print **exit-hole DOWN on the
  bed** (Z+ = drive flange). Same orientation as the v2 monolithic
  part. 4 mm brim around the small annular bottom contact, auto-
  supports for the funnel-cone underside and the top-cap bridges.

## Assembly

After printing, drilling, and tapping:

1. **Tap the shaft's M3 pilot hole** (top of stud) with an M3 hand tap.
2. **Slip the housing over the shaft from below** — the shaft's M3 stud
   passes up through the housing's central clearance bore in the drive
   flange (5 mm bore around 4 mm shaft → 0.5 mm radial slip fit, no
   bearings needed at this scale). The shaft's helix sits at 0.5 mm
   radial clearance against the rotating tube ID — clear of contact
   even with FDM tolerance stack-up.
3. **Anchor the M3 stud** (now protruding above the drive flange) to
   the stationary frame with an M3 nut + washer.
4. **Couple the spindle** to the drive flange OD (22 mm) via a
   set-screw shaft coupler — the flange has a flat machined into it for
   the grub screw to land on, preventing slip under torque.
5. Load powder through the four sectoral slots in the drive cap
   (around the shaft) and verify the housing rotates freely around the
   shaft before powering up.

## Printing on the Ender-3 from a USB stick

(Same procedure as v2 — repeat once per part.)

1. Format a USB stick as **FAT32**.
2. Copy **`SHAFT.gcode`** and **`HOUSING.gcode`** to the **root** of
   the stick. (These are the short-named PrusaSlicer Ender-3 copies —
   the stock LCD12864 truncates long filenames.)
3. Insert → *Print from media* → `SHAFT.gcode` → confirm. Repeat with
   `HOUSING.gcode`. Bed levelled, nozzle clean, PLA at 200/205 °C and
   bed at 60 °C (the file sets these — don't override on the LCD).
4. Each file's start block is `G28` → `G1 Z5 F5000` (real newlines —
   verified after the v2 ANSI-C-quoting fix).

If your Ender has the newer Creality 32-bit board / TFT (Ender-3 V2 /
S1 / Pro), the long-named files (`auger-shaft.Ender3.gcode`,
`auger-housing.Ender3.gcode`) work fine — the short-named copies are
defensive for stock 8-bit boards.
